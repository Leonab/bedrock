{{ _('Legislative Reform') }}

{% trans %} During the Reporting Period, we advocated for stronger encryption, privacy, and security practices. {% endtrans %} {% trans link_encryption='https://mzl.la/encrypt', link_principles='https://surveillance.mozilla.org', link_disclosure='https://blog.mozilla.org/blog/2016/05/11/advanced-disclosure-needed-to-keep-users-secure/' %} This included the running of a large scale education campaign on encryption, proposing Surveillance Principles for government adoption, and speaking out on the importance of disclosing security vulnerabilities to affected companies in judicial proceedings. {% endtrans %} {% trans link_brief='https://blog.mozilla.org/blog/2016/03/03/standing-up-with-apple-to-fight-for-everyones-security/', link_letter='http://www.digital4th.org/wp-content/uploads/2016/05/ECPA-Provider-Emergency-Letter.pdf' %} We also joined with other tech companies to file a brief, arguing against backdoors in the dispute between Apple and the FBI, and a letter to the U.S. Senate Judiciary Committee opposing legislation that would compel companies to disclose the content of user communications without judicial oversight. {% endtrans %}

{% trans link_broadband='https://blog.mozilla.org/netpolicy/files/2016/05/Comments-on-NPRM-for-Broadband-Privacy.pdf' %} In addition, we also commented on the proposed Investigatory Powers Bill (which would expand surveillance powers and undermine encryption in the United Kingdom) and on the U.S. Federal Communications Commission’s proposed broadband privacy framework. {% endtrans %} {% trans link_fund='https://blog.mozilla.org/blog/2016/06/09/help-make-open-source-secure/', link_leandata=url('mozorg.about.policy.lean-data') %} Lastly, we launched the Secure Open Source Fund to promote security auditing and patching of widely used open source technologies, as well as our Lean Data Initiative to encourage companies to be responsible in their use and stewardship of data. {% endtrans %}
